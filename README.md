# The Re-Entry Brief

**Bite-sized wisdom from Lenny's Podcast for product leaders returning after a career break.**

[Live site →](https://re-entybrief.netlify.app)

---

## What this is

Returning from parental leave, caregiving, or a sabbatical is hard. The podcast kept going. The industry kept moving. And nobody hands you a playbook for coming back.

The Re-Entry Brief is a distillation tool built on 289 episodes of [Lenny's Podcast](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/podcast) — one of the most trusted resources in product. It surfaces what matters for *your* specific return, without asking you to re-listen to 200+ hours of audio.

---

## Features

### What did I miss?
Select the dates you were away. Get one-paragraph takeaways from every episode that dropped during your leave — filterable by topic (AI, leadership, product, startups, and more). Read one in two minutes, or skim them all in fifteen.

### What nobody tells you about coming back
Quotes, stories, and frameworks from product leaders who've navigated re-entry firsthand: imposter syndrome, career pivots, invisible rules, and the things nobody prepares you for. Women make up nearly a third of Lenny's guests — but the conversation about career breaks, caregiving, and coming back after time away is almost absent from those episodes. We found it and brought it forward.

### Return path personalization
Four paths. Select yours and the content re-sorts instantly around what's most relevant:
- **Back to my old role** — re-entering where you left off
- **New job, new company** — open to something different
- **New role, same company** — moving internally with more flexibility
- **Part-time or flexible** — negotiating a different arrangement

### Frameworks for your return
Six mental models from the best product minds — The Hill Climb, Mental Time Travel, Put On the Coat, The Nevertheless, Assume You Decide, Gardener Not Builder — each tagged to the return paths where they're most applicable.

---

## Why I built this

I'm a product leader and this tool is built for people like me: returning after time away, trying to get back up to speed without drowning in content, and looking for advice that actually speaks to where we are.

Lenny's Podcast is outstanding, but it's long-form and not organized for re-entry. This is the version I wished existed when I came back.

---

## Data

Built from 289 episodes of Lenny's Podcast (authorized use). Takeaway coverage currently runs from Q1 2025 onward, with earlier quarters in progress. Women's wisdom quotes are sourced directly from episode transcripts with timestamp deep-links where available, so you can jump straight to the moment in the episode.
